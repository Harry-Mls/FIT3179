Pokies in Victoria: What Communities are Impacted the Most?

Electronic Gaming Machines (EGMs), known as slot machines globally and "pokies" in Australia, have been a prominent part of the nation's culture since their legalization in New South Wales in 1956. Originally introduced for entertainment in clubs and pubs, their popularity quickly spread nationwide. Today, Australia has one of the highest concentrations of pokies per capita. They are embedded in community venues like RSLs and sports clubs, often marketed as sources of local funding. However, growing concerns around gambling addiction and financial harm have led to calls for reform. Here we will investigate what Local Governement Area's (LGAs) are most heavily impacted, and the socio-economics of these communities.

The data highlights that LGAs that are more socio-economically disadvantaged have disproportionately high expenditure on pokies. This shows that the ones who lose the most to pokies are those who generally can't afford to. From this one one thing is clear: more can still be done to address this issue. Greater transparency, stronger regulation, and targeted support services are needed to protect vulnerable communities.
